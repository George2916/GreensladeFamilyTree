"Highway Engineer To Take Over Road - Greenslade Urging That Fill Be Made At Town Creek By County Commissioners", Milledgeville News, Milledgeville, Georgia, June 22, 1921
The article reads as follows:
     Division Engineer William Greenslade has announced the intention of the state and federal highway commission to take over and build up the road from Mt. Pelier Church to the Washington County line, and on to Sandersvillie, as soon as possible.
     The work on building up the road is being held up on account of Balwin County failing to put in a fill at Town creek, about thirteen miles out of Milledgeville on the Sandersville road, it is understood. A new modern type bridge was completed over Town creek some three or four months ago, though no effort is being made to complete the work of putting in the fill at the ends of the bridge and for such reason the highway commission engineer is complaining of being unable to start the work of building up the road.
     The road from Milledgeville to Sandersville is one of the most important highways leading out of the city and for such reason it is the purpose of Engineer Greenslade to commence work on it at the earliest date possible. It is understood that no promises have been made by the Baldwin county commissioners to put the finishing touches on the bridge work at Town creek at anything like an early date. Whether or not there is a possibility of the highway commission continuing to entertain the idea of putting in the extensive work on the road to the Washington line without the early cooperation of the Baldwin commissioners is not known.

"Funeral Services Of William Greenslade Today, Interment Will Be In Atlanta Saturday", Augusta Herald (Georgia), January 18, 1924
The article reads as follows:
     The funeral of William Greenslade will be held from the residence at Walton Way this afternoon at _____ o'clock and the interment will be in Atlanta. Dr. Joseph F. Sevier will officiate.
     Mr. Greenslade was state highway _____ engineer. He died suddenly in Chicago Tuesday at the Roosevelt Hotel.
     He was a Shriner and belonged to ____ Temple, Atlanta, the American Foreign Legion and Veterans of the ______ War. During the World War, he served with distinction as a captain of engineers.
     Mr. Greenslade is survived by his wife, Mrs. Louise Greenslade; father, George A. Greenslade; four brothers, _____ Greenslade, Jim Greenslade, _____ Greenslade and Dick Greenslade of Cuba; two sisters, Mrs. E. Towery and Mrs. O.C. Burnett, of Atlanta.
     The following gentlemen have been requested to act as pallbearers: G.C. Blount, E.C. Gartner, R.E. Barnes, F.P. VanStory, H.H. Hendrix and H.M. Sweat.
