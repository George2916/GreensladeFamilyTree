\n \t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Born February 21, 1912 in Chicago, Illinois.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Named after both her grandmothers.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Her best friend growing up was Mary Catherine Seipel (her future sister-in-law).

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Attended grade school in Scranton, Pennsylvania, Joan of Arc Junior High School in New York, New York and Julia Richman High School in New York, New YorK.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

She spent a number of her summers during her youth at West Point, where her mother (or aunt) was good friends with the supervisor of the grounds.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Worked for Montgomery Ward for two years in a clerical position after high school, took nine years off to raise her children, returned to work in 1943 for Western Union as a telegram operator for ten years and then worked at Bethlehem Steel for 21 years as a secretary.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Died April 11, 2010 in Raleigh, North Carolina of congestive heart failure.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Buried in Palm Beach Memorial Park in Lantana, Florida in same plot as Richard Alfred Greenslade (husband), Albert Llewellen Morrison (brother) and Margaret \"Marge\" Jaynes (sister-in-law).
