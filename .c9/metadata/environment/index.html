Viewing Tips
\n\t\t\t\t\t\n\t \t\t\t
\n\t \t\t\t
\n\t \t\t\t\t
  1. The family trees are best viewed on a Chrome browser.
    2. \n\t \t\t\t\t\t
  2. The family trees are very wide and are best viewed on a laptop or pc.
    3. \n\t \t\t\t\t\t
  3. Scroll vertically and horizontally over the family trees by using the scroll bars on the right and bottom of the screen.
    4. \n\t \t\t\t\t\t
  4. To see more of the data in a family tree on the screen, consider using the zoom function on your browser menu.

Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tThou art more lovely and more temperate:
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tRough winds do shake the darling buds of May,
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tAnd summer’s lease hath all too short a date;
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tSometime too hot the eye of heaven shines,
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tAnd often is his gold complexion dimm'd;
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tAnd every fair from fair sometime declines,
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tBy chance or nature’s changing course untrimm'd;
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tBut thy eternal summer shall not fade,
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tNor lose possession of that fair thou ow’st;
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tNor shall death brag thou wander’st in his shade,
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tWhen in eternal lines to time thou grow’st:
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tSo long as men can breathe or eyes can see,
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tSo long lives this website, and this website
\n\t\t\t\t\t\tgives life to thee.

\n\t\t\t\t\t\tWilliam Shakespeare, Sonnet 18

Extract from 1851 census of Bishop's Nympton ↗

The census shows William Greenslade, age 58, was the unmarried head of a household whose occupation was farmer of 100 acres. The household included his unmarried brother Henry Greenslade, age 50, and his nephew John Greenslade, age 19, both of whom were employed on the farm, as well as a servant named Elizabeth Lowdon and an apprentice named Emma Hawks.

Born October 18, 1864 in Bishop's Nympton, county of Devon, England. Birth certificate unable to be located in St. Catherine's House in London.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

A mystery: Why do the registrations of baptism of his daughters Carolina and Georgiana state that his parents are Juan Alberto and Catalina Maria Greenslade of New York, York?

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Emigrated to USA in 1875.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Naturalized as a US citizen on May 18, 1908 in County of Spartanburg, South Carolina.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Married Eva Josephine Green in 1897 in Matachin, Panama.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Remarried Eva Josephine Green on September 11, 1913 in Callidonia corregimiento in Panama City, Panama.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Work history: \n\t\t\t\t\t\t

  • 1896 - 1901: conductor then roadmaster for Panama Railroad.
    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t
  • 1901 - 1903: Superintendent of Construction for Cuba Central Railroad.
    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t
  • 1903 - 1907: Guayaquil and Quito Railway, Ecuador.
    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t
  • June 1907 - 1910: superindendent, then General Superindendent of Constuction in the Central Division of the Panama Canal.
    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t
  • 1911 - ? : Madeira-Mamoré Railway, Brazil.
    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t
  • 1914 - 1929: United Fruit Company in Cuba.
    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t
  • 1929 - 1930: United Fruit Company in Panama.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Recipient of the Roosevelt Medal, awarded to American citizens who completed at least two years of service on the Panama Canal. 🡥

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Acquired property in Salisbury, Maryland in May 1930.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Died July 6, 1947 in Salisbury, Maryland of congestive heart failure.

\n\t\t\t\t\t

Buried in Wicomico Memorial Park in Salisbury in same plot as Eva Josephine Green (wife), Carolina Virginia Greenslade (daughter) and Georgiana Ancia Greenslade (daughter).
