First generation

Harry W. Knight

Born in England in October 1845.

His daughter Annie' registration of marriage gives his first name as Albert; his daughter Jessica's death certificate gives his first name as William; according to Eleanor Martha Morrison (his granddaugher), his first name was Harry. In addition, the 1900 census of Vanceboro, Maine and the marriage certificate of his daughter Lillian indicate that his name was Harry W. Knight.

According to the marriage certificate of his daughter Lillian, he was born in England and in 1907 he was a baggage master living in Vanceboro, Maine. This is consistent with the information contained in the 1900 census of Vanceboro, Maine.

Extract from the 1900 census of Vanceboro, Maine ↗

The census indicates that Harry W. Knight, age 54, was the head of a household that include his wife, Sarah E. Knight, age 52, his daughters Lillian J., age 18, and Jessica L., age 16, and his son Willie E., age 14. The census also indicates that Harry W. and Sarah E. Knight had been married for 25 years in 1900.

Sarah (or Jane) Patterson

The marriage certificate of her daughter Anne indicates that her name was Sarah Jane. The marriage certificate of her daughter Lillian indicates that her first name was Sarah and the death certificate of Lillian indicates that here first name was Jane. However, according to Eleanor Martha Morrison, her granddaughter, her first name was either Eleanor or Martha.

According to the marriage certificate of her daughter Lillian, Sarah Patterson was born in St. John, province of New Brunswick, Canada and in 1907 she was living in Vanceboro, Maine.

The 1900 census of Vanceboro, Maine states that her father was born in England and her mother was born in Scotland. This census is available under "More info" of her husband.

Lillian Irene Knight

Her exact date of birth is unclear. Her burial record states that she was born April 12, 1882. Her marriage certificate indicates that she was 25 years old when she died in 1907 (implying she was born in about 1882), but her death certificate indicates that she was 29 when she died in 1914 (implying she was born in about 1885). According to Eleanor Martha Morrison, her niece, she was younger than Jessica Loretta Knight (born 1884), her sister, suggesting the death certificate is accurate on this point. Moreover, Jessica was born in New Brunswick, Canada and Lillian was born in Maine. It seems unlikely the family would have crossed back into Canada to give birth to Jessica if Jessica was born after Lillian.

According to the Record Of A Marriage (see Documents tab), she was born in Vanceboro, Maine and she was a school teacher at the time of her marriage.

Died June 22, 1914 in Millinocket, Maine of a pulmonary embolism.

Buried in Maple Cemetery in Danforth, Maine.

Eleanor Martha Morrison

Born February 21, 1912 in Chicago, Illinois.

She said that she was named after both her grandmothers - i.e., one grandmother was named Eleanor and the other was named Martha.

Her best friend growing up was Mary Catherine Seipel (her future sister-in-law).

Attended grade school in Scranton, Pennsylvania, Joan of Arc Junior High School in New York, New York and Julia Richman High School in New York, New YorK.

She spent a number of her summers during her youth at West Point, where her mother (or aunt) was good friends with the supervisor of the grounds.

Worked for Montgomery Ward for two years in a clerical position after high school, took nine years off to raise her children, returned to work in 1943 for Western Union as a telegram operator for ten years and then worked at Bethlehem Steel for 21 years as a secretary.

Died April 11, 2010 in Raleigh, North Carolina of congestive heart failure.

Buried in Palm Beach Memorial Park in Lantana, Florida in same plot as Richard "Dick" Alfred Greenslade (husband), Albert Llewellen Morrison (brother) and Margaret "Marge" Mary Jaynes (sister-in-law).

Susan Lina Morrison

Graduated from St. Lawrence University.

Named an honorary alumna of SUNY Potsdam in 2016. ↗
Text of the article reads as follows:

"SUNY Potsdam Names Susan L. Morrison an Honorary Alumna
Longtime Supporter Susan L. Morrison is Given Honorary Lifetime Membership in SUNY Potsdam Alumni Association

POTSDAM, NY (07/21/2016)-- The SUNY Potsdam Alumni Association recently honored Susan L. Morrison with Honorary Lifetime Membership during the College's Bicentennial Reunion Weekend.

Honorary Lifetime Membership is presented to non-alums who have made exceptional contributions to the SUNY Potsdam Alumni Association and/or the College. Morrison was recognized for her commitment to SUNY Potsdam, her generous support of students and her strong belief in the promise of a Potsdam education.

Along with her partner, Anna Ashwood Collins '67, Morris has had a tremendous impact upon SUNY Potsdam and its students. A loyal donor and supporter of Potsdam for more than 25 years, her affinity for the College has grown steadily. In 2009, Morrison and Collins re-engaged with the College in meaningful ways, attending regional alumni gatherings and taking visits from the College in New York City. Despite being a graduate of St. Lawrence University, Canton, Morrison's appreciation for SUNY Potsdam was evident, as was her mutual desire to explore how she and Collins could positively impact the College and student experiences.

In 2010, the couple established the Anna Ashwood Collins '67 and Susan L. Morrison Scholarship to provide a significant scholarship to students with financial need. Their gift has already had a meaningful impact upon its recipients. The delight derived from witnessing the impact of their generosity is what resonates most with Morrison, so much so that she and Collins make it a point to do their very best to return to Potsdam each year to attend the annual Scholarship Luncheon. Here, they have the opportunity to meet and visit with student recipients and their families.

Fortified by their experiences with their recipients and confident in the promise of their gift's impact, the couple notified the College of their intention to enhance their scholarship through a bequest. Once realized, this planned gift will fund eight significant student scholarships in perpetuity at the College. This generous gift, one of the largest in SUNY Potsdam's 200-year history, was also responsible for taking the College over its fundraising goal of $32 million for Take the Lead: The Campaign for Potsdam."

Anna Ashwood Collins

Graduated from SUNY Potsdam with a BA in 1967 and an MA in 1969.

Author of the Abby Doyle series of mystery novels.

From nny360.com (news source for northern New York State): ↗

"Anna Ashwood Collins of New York, New York, passed away on May 8th, 2021, peacefully at age 82. She is survived by her spouse Susan L. Morrison. Anna was pre-deceased by her parents Clara (Dickerson) and Clarence Ashwood and husband George Collins.

Originally from Morristown, Anna also resided in Gouverneur, Canton, Queens, NY and lived for years in the beauty of Jekyll Island, GA.

Anna attended Gouverneur High School and graduated with her BA in English (67’) and MA of Science Degree in Education (69’) from SUNY at Potsdam, where, with Susan Morrison, they built a generous legacy of giving.

During her long career, Anna worked as a Reporter for the Syracuse Post Standard, the Watertown Times, and the St. Lawrence Plain Dealer. A voracious reader and writer, Anna was the author of several Mysteries, a genre she loved. Over the years Anna penned numerous reviews and articles for The Mystery Book Review, guiding fans to the next great page-turner. Reading was a gift to Anna and she shared that gift with everyone she knew. She penned multiple columns for the Jekyll Golden Islander, many that circulated nationally. If that’s not enough for one lifetime, Anna was a contributor to Jacques Cousteau’s Anthology.

Anna loved the world and everything around her, and her passions were multiple. She loved cars, fast cars and sports cars. As a young girl, her father, an operating engineer, taught Anna everything about the automotive universe, spurring a lifetime hobby and interest.

Travel was another passion for Anna, she loved cruises and cherished her many trips to Hawaii, a destination she recounted about time and time again to her friends. During her travels, she also had the opportunity to pursue another long-time hobby – bird watching. Seeing a new-feathered friend in Jekyll Island, Hawaii, Upstate New York or Central Park was always a thrill for Anna. An athlete, she golfed and enjoyed bowling and tennis.

Music and theatre were additional fascinations for Anna. From tuning into Nashville’s Grand Ole Opry every chance she could, to attending concerts, Jazz festivals and cabaret performances, Anna loved the majesty of the of live music and theatre. Music was magic for Anna and she could never attend too many performances.

Some would be surprised that Anna served for 5 years as a member of the New York City Police Department Auxiliary Police, but then again they did not know Anna, she could do anything she set her mind to.

Anna will be missed by so many. In honor of her incredible legacy, memorial contributions may be made to the Potsdam College Foundation, 44 Pierrepont Avenue, Potsdam, NY 13676. All contributions will be added to the Anna Ashwood Collins ’67 & Susan L. Morrison Scholars Endowment, where Anna’s legacy will live in perpetuity and be remembered by the next generation of SUNY Potsdam alumnus."
