The census shows George Greenslade, age 26, was the unmarried head of a household whose occupation was butcher. The household included (1) Jane Baker, age 25, who is referred to as an unmarried housekeeper, and (2) Henry Greenslade, age 60, an unmarried agricultural laborer.

The census shows George Greenslade, age 38, was the married head of a household whose occupation was butcher. George Alfred Greenslade

Born October 18, 1864 in Bishop's Nympton, county of Devon, England. Birth certificate unable to be located in St. Catherine's House in London.

A mystery: Why do the registrations of baptism of his daughters Carolina and Georgiana state that his parents are Juan Alberto and Catalina Maria Greenslade of New York, York?

Emigrated to USA in 1875.

Naturalized as a US citizen on May 18, 1908 in County of Spartanburg, South Carolina.

Married Eva Josephine Green in 1897 in Matachin, Panama.

Remarried Eva Josephine Green on September 11, 1913 in Callidonia corregimiento in Panama City, Panama.

Work history:

  • 1896 - 1901: conductor then roadmaster for Panama Railroad.
  • 1901 - 1903: Superintendent of Construction for Cuba Central Railroad.
  • 1903 - 1907: Guayaquil and Quito Railway, Ecuador.
  • June 1907 - 1910: superindendent, then General Superindendent of Constuction in the Central Division of the Panama Canal.
  • 1911 - ? : Madeira-Mamoré Railway, Brazil.
  • 1914 - 1929: United Fruit Company in Cuba.
  • 1929 - 1930: United Fruit Company in Panama.

Recipient of the Roosevelt Medal, awarded to American citizens who completed at least two years of service on the Panama Canal. ↗

Acquired property in Salisbury, Maryland in May 1930.

Died July 6, 1947 in Salisbury, Maryland of congestive heart failure.

Buried in Wicomico Memorial Park in Salisbury in same plot as Eva Josephine Green (wife), Carolina Virginia Greenslade (daughter) and Georgiana Ancia Greenslade (daughter).
